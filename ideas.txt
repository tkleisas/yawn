=== VIDEO & PROCEDURAL GRAPHICS ===

Two new track types mirroring the audio/MIDI paradigm:
  - Video Track (like audio): plays video file clips with trim/loop/fade
  - Procedural Track (like MIDI): runs GLSL fragment shaders as visual instruments

Visual effects chain: post-processing shaders on video/procedural tracks (blur, color grade, etc.)
Visual mixer: layer compositing with opacity, blend modes (add, multiply, screen, overlay)
Master visual output: renders to a separate SDL3 window (multi-window already supported)

Audio-reactive uniforms: feed FFT bins, RMS, transient triggers, beat phase into shaders
Automation: route existing automation system to shader uniform parameters (knobs control visuals)

Shader engine: hot-reloadable GLSL with standard interface (iTime, iBeat, iFFT[], iTransient, custom params)
Video decode: FFmpeg/libav on separate thread, frame textures uploaded to GPU
Timing: video frames synced to transport position (not wall clock), scrubbing/looping works

Compositing: layer stack with blend modes, or future node graph
